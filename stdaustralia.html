Why Study in AUSTRALIA

Australia is one of the top choices for international students due to its excellent education system, vibrant culture, and welcoming communities. Here are some key reasons to consider studying in Australia:

High-Quality Education

Australia is home to globally recognized universities such as the University of Sydney, University of Melbourne, and Australian National University (ANU). These institutions are renowned for their cutting-edge research and diverse academic programs.

Affordable Education

While tuition fees in Australia are competitive, scholarships and financial aid options are widely available for international students, making it an accessible destination for quality education.

Post-Graduation Work Opportunities

Australia offers a Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485), allowing graduates to work and gain professional experience after completing their studies. This is a stepping stone toward permanent residency for many.

Cultural Diversity

Australia is a multicultural country with a vibrant student community. This diversity creates an enriching experience, enabling students to meet people from all over the world.

World-Class Research Opportunities

Australia invests heavily in research, especially in fields like medicine, engineering, and environmental science. International students can benefit from state-of-the-art facilities and innovative projects.

Required Documents

Documents required for University Application:

  • Scan copy of your passport
  • Scan copies (original) of all educational Certificates & Marksheets
  • Scan copies (original) of Two Academic Reference Letters (For PG)
  • Statement of Purpose
  • Scan copy (original) of UKVI/Academic IELTS Certificate
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g., extracurricular activities)

Documents required for Visa Application:

  • A valid Passport
  • A completed Online Visa Application Form
  • Appropriate Visa Application Fee
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)
  • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) Letter from your sponsor (University)
  • Medical Test - IOM
  • Bank Statement and Bank Solvency Certificate (from Listed Bank only)
  • All educational Certificates and Marksheets (Original)
  • IELTS/TOEFL Certificate
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g extracurricular activities)

Application Procedures

Intakes

Australian universities generally offer two main intakes:

  • February (Semester 1)
  • July (Semester 2)
  • Some universities also offer November intake for selected programs.

English Language Requirements

  • Undergraduate: IELTS 6.0–6.5 (or equivalent)
  • Postgraduate: IELTS 6.5–7.0 (or equivalent)

Academic Requirement

Undergraduate – Bachelors

  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (out of 4.0) in high school
  • strong performance in relevant subjects
  • Some programs may require SAT or ACT scores.

Postgraduate – Masters

    • A minimum CGPA of 2.5–3.0 for most programs.
    • GRE/GMAT may be required for certain fields like business or engineering.
    • For MBA programs at some Universities, students need to have work experience along with GMAT score.

Visa Procedure and Fees

To apply for an Australian Student Visa (Subclass 500), you must provide:

  • Proof of Acceptance: Confirmation of Enrollment (CoE) from a registered university.
  • Proof of Financial Capacity: Show funds covering tuition fees, living costs (AUD 21,041/year), and return travel.
  • Health Insurance: Obtain Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC).
  • Visa Fee: Approximately AUD 650.
  • Processing Time: 4–8 weeks, depending on the application and time of year.

Financial Requirements for Study Permit:

  • Tuition Fees: AUD 20,000–45,000 per year (varies by course and university).
  • Living Expenses: AUD 21,041 per year (minimum requirement set by the Australian government).

Visa Application Processing Time for Bangladesh:

  • Normal Processing: 8–12 weeks.
  • Priority Processing: Available at an additional fee for expedited processing.

Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP):

After completing your studies, you may be eligible for the Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485), which allows you to work in Australia for up to 2–4 years, depending on your qualification level. This experience can help in transitioning to permanent residency.
