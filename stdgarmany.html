STUDY IN GERMANY

Are you considering studying in Germany from Bangladesh? MASJT is here to help you select the right program and university to achieve your academic and professional goals. Work with our experienced consultants and graduates who have firsthand knowledge of German education.

Why Study in GERMANY

Germany is one of the most popular study destinations for international students, thanks to its world-class education, innovative research opportunities, and low or no tuition fees at public universities. Here are the top reasons to study in Germany:

World-Class Education

Germany is home to some of the best universities globally, such as Technical University of Munich (TUM), Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich (LMU), and Heidelberg University. The focus on academic excellence and practical learning ensures a well-rounded education.

Low or No Tuition Fees

Most public universities in Germany offer tuition-free education for both domestic and international students, requiring only a minimal semester contribution.

English-Taught Programs

Germany offers a wide range of English-taught bachelor’s, master’s, and Ph.D. programs, making it accessible for non-German speakers.

Strong Job Market and Post-Study Opportunities

Germany is Europe's largest economy, providing ample job opportunities in industries like engineering, IT, business, and healthcare. Graduates can apply for an 18-month post-study work visa to find employment in Germany.

Cutting-Edge Research and Innovation

With a strong emphasis on research and innovation, German universities are ideal for students interested in science, technology, and engineering fields.

Affordable Living Costs

Although Germany is a developed country, the cost of living is manageable, especially for students in smaller cities.

Required Documents

Documents required for University Application:

  • Scan copy of your passport
  • Scan copies (original) of all educational Certificates & Marksheets
  • Scan copies (original) of Two Academic Reference Letters (For PG)
  • Statement of Purpose
  • Scan copy (original) of UKVI/Academic IELTS Certificate
  • Proof of English or German language proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL or TestDaF/DSH)
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g., extracurricular activities)

Documents required for Visa Application:

  • A valid Passport
  • A completed Online Visa Application Form
  • Appropriate Visa Application Fee
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)
  • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) Letter from your sponsor (University)
  • Medical Test - IOM
  • Proof of accommodation in Germany
  • Bank Statement and Bank Solvency Certificate (from Listed Bank only)
  • All educational Certificates and Marksheets (Original)
  • IELTS/TOEFL Certificate
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g extracurricular activities)

Application Procedures

Intakes

German universities generally have two main intakes:

  • Winter Semester (October): Main intake
  • Summer Semester (April): Secondary intake for select programs

Language Requirements

  • English-Taught Programs: IELTS (6.0–7.0) or TOEFL (80–100)
  • German-Taught Programs: TestDaF (Level 4), DSH-2, or equivalent

Academic Requirement

Undergraduate – Bachelors

  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (out of 4.0) in high school
  • strong performance in relevant subjects

Postgraduate – Masters

    • A minimum CGPA of 2.5–3.0 for most programs.
    • Specific programs may require entrance exams or interviews.

Visa Procedure and Fees

To apply for an German Student Visa (Subclass 500), you must provide:

  • Receive your admission letter from a recognized German university.
  • Open a blocked account with €11,208 to demonstrate financial sufficiency.
  • Submit the visa application with all required documents.
  • Attend the visa interview at the German Embassy or Consulate.

Tuition Fees and Living Costs:
    Tuition Fees:
  • Public Universities: Tuition-free (only a semester contribution of €100–€350)
  • Private Universities: €10,000–€20,000 per year.
    Living Expenses:
  • Estimated monthly expenses: €850–€1,200 (includes rent, food, transportation, and health insurance).

Accommodation Options:

  • University dormitories: €200–€400 per month
  • Private apartments: €500–€800 per month

Visa Application Processing Time for Bangladesh:

  • Normal Processing: 8–12 weeks.
  • Priority Processing: Available at an additional fee for expedited processing.

Post-Graduation Opportunities:

After completing your studies, you can apply for an 18-month residence permit to find a job in Germany. Graduates from German universities have high employability in fields like engineering, IT, healthcare, and business.
