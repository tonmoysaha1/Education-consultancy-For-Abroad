Why Study in CANADA

Canada is one of the top study destinations for international students. Known for its welcoming environment, high-quality education system, and diverse culture, here are a few reasons why studying in Canada is an excellent choice:

High-Quality Education

Canada is home to some of the world's top universities and offers a world-class education recognized globally. Institutions such as the University of Toronto, McGill University, and the University of British Columbia are known for their academic excellence and innovative research programs.

Affordable Education

Compared to other popular study destinations like the USA or the UK, tuition fees in Canada are relatively affordable. There are also various scholarships and financial aid options available for international students.

Post-Graduation Work Opportunities

Canada offers Post-Graduation Work Permits (PGWP), allowing international graduates to work in Canada after completing their studies. This is a great opportunity to gain valuable work experience and possibly transition to permanent residency.

Cultural Diversity

Canada is a multicultural country that welcomes people from all over the world. This provides an excellent opportunity for students to experience different cultures, make new friends, and develop global connections.

Required Documents

Documents required for University Application:

  • Scan copy of your passport
  • Scan copies (original) of all educational Certificates & Marksheets
  • Scan copies (original) of Two Academic Reference Letters (For PG)
  • Statement of Purpose
  • Scan copy (original) of UKVI/Academic IELTS Certificate
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g., extracurricular activities)

Documents required for Tier 4 Visa Application:

  • A valid Passport
  • A completed Online Visa Application Form
  • Appropriate Visa Application Fee
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)
  • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) Letter from your sponsor (University)
  • Medical Test - IOM
  • Bank Statement and Bank Solvency Certificate (from Listed Bank only)
  • All educational Certificates and Marksheets (Original)
  • IELTS/TOEFL Certificate
  • Job experience letter (if required)
  • Other supporting documents (if available, e.g extracurricular activities)

Application Procedures

Intakes

  • The main intake periods for Canadian universities are Fall (September)
  • Winter (January)
  • Spring (May)
  • Some universities may also offer a Summer intake.

English Language Requirements

  • Most universities require proof of English language proficiency.
  • For undergraduate programs, IELTS scores of 6.0–7.0 are commonly required.
  • While postgraduate programs typically require scores of 6.5–7.5.

Academic Requirement

Undergraduate – Bachelors

  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (out of 4.0) in high school
  • strong performance in relevant subjects
  • Some programs may require SAT or ACT scores.

Postgraduate – Masters

    • A minimum CGPA of 2.5–3.0 for most programs.
    • Some graduate programs may require GRE or GMAT scores (especially for business, engineering, or computer science).
    • For MBA programs at some Universities, students need to have work experience along with GMAT score.

    Each institution sets its own entry requirements, and you will find that they vary between universities. Your application will normally be assessed on your academic level of qualification. To make sure you meet all the admissions criteria for the course(s) you wish to apply for, contact the international office of the institutions you are applying to and check.

Visa Procedure and Fees

To apply for a Canadian study permit, you must meet the following requirements:

  • Proof of Acceptance: A letter of acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada.
  • Proof of Funds: You must show sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and return transportation.
  • Visa Fees: The application fee for a Canadian study permit is approximately CAD 150.
  • Visa Processing Time: Generally, processing takes 2 to 3 months, depending on your home country and the time of year.

Financial Requirements for Study Permit:

  • Tuition Fees: CAD 7,000 to CAD 29,000 per year (depending on the program and university).
  • Living Expenses: Estimated at CAD 10,000 to CAD 15,000 per year, depending on the city.

Visa Application Processing Time for Bangladesh:

  • Normal Processing: 8–12 weeks.
  • Priority Processing: Available at an additional fee for expedited processing.

Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP):

After completing your studies, you may apply for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP), which allows you to work in Canada for up to 3 years, depending on the length of your program. This is an excellent opportunity to gain Canadian work experience, which can be valuable if you want to apply for permanent residency.
