Pre-Departure Support Program

If MASJT Education Consultancy is offering pre-departure support to students, it is crucial to ensure that the support covers all essential aspects of their journey and initial transition. Here's a structured plan for pre-departure support tailored for international students:

Documentation and Visa Assistance

Ensure Documentation Readiness
  • Verify that students have their passports, visas, and study permits.
  • Provide guidance on any country-specific immigration requirements.
Flight Details
  • Assist students with booking flights and understanding layovers or transit processes.
Travel Insurance
  • Educate students on the importance of travel and health insurance.
  • Provide recommendations for affordable and reliable providers.

Accommodation and Arrival

Accommodation Guidance
  • Help students book university dormitories, private housing, or temporary stay options.
  • Provide tips on securing affordable and safe housing.
Airport Pickup/Transportation
  • Coordinate or recommend airport pickup services at the destination.
  • Offer instructions on public transport systems or taxi services.

Orientation to Destination Country

Cultural Awareness
  • Organize webinars or provide handbooks about cultural norms, etiquettes, and social expectations.
Weather and Packing Tips
  • Advise students on what to pack based on the climate and season.
  • Suggest essential items for academic and personal life.
Currency and Banking
  • Explain currency exchange processes.
  • Guide students on opening a bank account and transferring funds internationally.

Health and Wellbeing

Vaccinations and Medical Checkups
  • Remind students about required vaccinations or health screenings.
Health Insurance
  • Explain the health insurance requirements of the destination country.
Mental Health Support
  • Discuss coping with homesickness and stress and suggest university counseling services.

Academic Preparation

Academic Resources
  • Share information about textbooks, online resources, and preparatory courses.
Understanding University Systems
  • Familiarize students with grading systems, credit hours, and academic integrity rules.
Time Management Skills
  • Offer workshops on balancing studies, work (if applicable), and personal life.

Communication and Networking

Stay Connected
  • Recommend local SIM cards or international phone plans for connectivity.
Connect with Alumni/Peers
  • Facilitate group chats or forums for students heading to the same country or institution.
  • Organize Q&A sessions with alumni for tips and guidance.

Emergency Preparedness

Emergency Contacts
  • Provide a list of important contacts, such as embassy details, university emergency numbers, and local law enforcement.
